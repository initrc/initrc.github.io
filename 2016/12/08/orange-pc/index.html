Orange PC

2016.12.8

I built a PC!

  • To play the games I bought on Steam but never played.
  • I played all the Vita games that I liked.
  • There’s no PS4 only game that I liked yet, and PC has better graphics.
  • I might try 3D modeling and build games later.
  • Should be fun.

It turned out that building a PC IS quite fun, and building a small form factor PC is challenging too.

Here’s the part list.

  • CPU: i5 6500 (enough for gaming)
  • CPU cooler: CRYORIG C7 (quiet and does the job)
  • Motherboard: GA-B150N Phoenix-WIFI (supports sata M.2 ssd)
  • Memory: Crucial Ballistix Sport LT 16GB (great deal and it’s red)
  • Storage: Crucial MX300 525GB M.2-2280 (M.2 saves sata and power cables)
  • GPU: Gigabyte GeForce GTX 1070 8GB Windforce OC (great cooling, and it comes with Watch Dogs 2)
  • Case: Fractal Design Node 202 (the only console form factor case that looks good)
  • Power supply: Corsair SF 450W 80+ Gold (quiet, fully modular with flat cables)
  • Case fan: Cougar Vortex PWM 70.5 CFM 120mm Fan (x2, quiet, high static pressure, and orange)
  • Other: Steam controller (great with steam big picture mode)

I didn’t care about the color scheme because the case doesn’t have a window and it sits in the TV unit. But as I was ordering hardware pieces, it turned out to have an orange color scheme. The motherboard has orange LEDs, the graphics card has some orange marks, and the case fans are orange too. You can see the case fans through the mesh if the case is placed vertically. The Node 202 case comes with a PSU but the cables are ugly and it’s not modular. So I bought the one without the PSU and got the Corsair PSU. All cables are black. Its fans is larger than the Silverstone PSU so it’s quieter.

Since the case is really small, it’s hard to keep it cool. Originally I was planning to get a blower style GPU so it blows the hot air out of the case, instead of relying on case fans. But all of the blower style GPUs are loud. So instead I got the Cougar high static pressure case fans. They are really amazing! The maximum GPU temperature is only 71℃ during the Unigine Valley benchmark. Thanks to the high quality fans (case fans, CPU cooler, PSU fans), the system is pretty quiet.

The case has one drawback though. It comes with a GPU supporting bracket that helps holding the GPU in both horizontal and vertical placement. But after I mounted two case fans, there’s no place for the supporting bracket. I DIY’ed one with some post-it, a ribbon and a hole punch. It works fine in the horizontal placement.
